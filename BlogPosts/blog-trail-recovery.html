Trail Recovery Tips

Hiking or trail running can put a lot of stress on your body. Proper recovery ensures that you stay strong, reduce soreness, and avoid injuries. Here are our top recovery tips:

1. Stretching After Trails

Focus on hamstrings, calves, and hip flexors for at least 10 minutes after every hike or run.

2. Foam Rolling

Roll your quads, IT bands, and glutes to relieve muscle tension and improve blood flow.

3. Hydration and Nutrition

Drink plenty of water and refuel with balanced meals rich in protein and complex carbs.

4. Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep to maximize muscle repair and overall recovery.

5. Active Recovery

On non-hiking days, go for a short walk, do light mobility exercises, or perform yoga.
