Top 5 Exercises for Hikers and Van-Life Adventurers

Hiking and living life on the road require strength, mobility, and endurance. Whether you're tackling steep trails or staying active between long drives, these five exercises are designed to help you stay strong and injury-free.

1. Step-Ups

Build leg power and stability by stepping onto a bench or sturdy surface with added weight, such as a loaded backpack.

2. Split Squats

Improve balance and single-leg strength, essential for uneven trails and long hikes.

3. Plank Shoulder Taps

Enhance core stability, which is critical for carrying packs or maintaining good posture during van-life workouts.

4. Resistance Band Rows

Strengthen your back and shoulders with a lightweight, packable resistance band — perfect for training on the go.

5. Glute Bridges

Activate and strengthen your glutes to prevent knee pain during long hikes and descents.
